The Hill Garden & Pergola

A hidden 230-metre Edwardian raised walkway on the edge of Hampstead Heath, built for Lord Leverhulme in 1904. Stone columns, wisteria, climbing roses, overgrown arches overlooking a pond. The entrance feels like you're trespassing on someone's property. Free, and barely anyone knows it exists.

Hill Garden & Pergola

Hampstead Heath · Free Entry · Built 1904

April is the best time to visit — wisteria in full bloom, draping everything in colour. It was the private garden of a now-demolished manor house. Opened to the public in 1963.

Feels like walking through an abandoned palace overgrown by beautiful plants, flowers and vines. — Google Review · 5★
Stunning photo opportunities with the wisteria hanging from the pergola. Absolutely worth a visit. — Google Review · 5★
18:45 – 19:45

Sunset at Parliament Hill

98 metres up, one of London's 13 legally protected views. The Shard, St Paul's, The Gherkin, Canary Wharf — the whole skyline. Sit down, eat the snacks, and watch the sky go stupid colours. Performatively. Chingiz-style. But better.

Sunset: 19:35. Golden hour from ~18:45.

Parliament Hill

Hampstead Heath · 98m · Protected View

One of London's highest points. On a clear day you can see from the Palace of Westminster to Canary Wharf. Benches at the top, or just sit on the grass.

Choose a nice day, preferably an hour before sunset, and walk up. Dawn and dusk create special spectacles. — Tripadvisor · 5★
Couples sharing cozy conversations, individuals taking in the calm breeze. Undeniably romantic. — Google Review · 5★
